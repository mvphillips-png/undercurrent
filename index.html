Field Guide

Every conversation has a hidden layer.
This draws it.

There's what was said — and then there's what was actually going on. The weird pause. The enthusiasm that felt slightly wrong. The question nobody asked out loud. Undercurrent reads that second layer and maps it like a gravity field, because that's what it is: invisible forces, pulling on everyone in the room.

The five forces
Pull

Drawing toward. Wanting, attachment, needing something from someone. The reason you keep re-reading their last message.

Pressure

Pushing. Demands, obligation, "just circling back on this!" for the fourth time. Weight applied on purpose.

Avoidance

Pulling away. The dodge, the subject change, the person who went quiet three messages ago and hopes nobody noticed.

Performance

Acting a feeling that isn't real. "SO excited!!" from someone who is not excited. Enthusiasm worn like a costume.

Tension

Unresolved friction. The thing between two people that neither will name — so it just sits there, humming.

When to use it

Before you reply to that message. After the meeting that went sideways and you can't say why. When you're the group-chat historian trying to figure out where it turned. Or on a scene you're writing — if the field looks boring, the scene probably is too.

Fair warning: once you can see the field, you can't unsee it.
